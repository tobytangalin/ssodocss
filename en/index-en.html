Walkthrough: Configuring SAML single sign-on

Abstract

Describes how to set up an SAML SSO connection in the Sitecore Cloud Portal.

Before you begin

  • You must be an Organization Admin or Organization Owner in your Sitecore Cloud Portal organization.

  • You must have the ability to register Sitecore Cloud Portal with your identity provider (IdP).

  • Your IdP must support SAML 1.1 or SAML 2.0.

Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) is an open security standard for managing authentication and access. SAML for single sign-on allows team members to log in to the Sitecore Cloud Portal using their existing SAML identity provider.

Sitecore supports service provider (SP) initiated SAML.

Note

Sitecore does not support just-in-time (JIT) provisioning, single logout (SLO), or SAML signing certificates.

In SAML single sign-on setup (SSO), there are two main entities:

  • Service provider (SP) - The app or website that a user wants to access. In our case, Sitecore Cloud Portal is the SP.

  • Identity provider (IdP) - The entity that stores user information and credentials and provides authentication. Popular IdPs include Microsoft Entra and Okta.

To allow team members to log in using an SAML identity provider, you need to configure SAML SSO.

When setting up SAML SSO, you'll need SP metadata to configure your IdP and you'll need IdP metadata to configure your SSO connection. Sitecore generates the the SP metadata when you add a new connection. Your IdP usually generates the IdP metadata when you create a SAML app to register Sitecore Cloud Portal with your IdP.

Add a new SSO connection

When you add a new SAML connection, Sitecore generates metadata that you use to register Sitecore Cloud Portal in your identity provider.

  1. Navigate to the Sitecore Cloud Portal SSO page and click Add SSO connection.

  2. In the drop-down menu, click Use SAML.

  3. In the Add SAML connection dialog, enter the email domain for your connection, then click Add and configure.

  4. Copy the Service provider (SP) metadata URL, Audience URI, and Assertion Consumer Service (ACS) URL. You'll need this when you register Sitecore Cloud Portal with your identity provider.

Register Sitecore Cloud Portal with your SAML identity provider

To configure SAML SSO, you need to register Sitecore Cloud Portal with your identity provider. This process varies depending on the identity provider, but in general, you must:

  1. Create a SAML app in your IdP (some providers call it an app integration, client, or SAML profile).

    Example: Ping Identity

  2. Configure your SAML app with the service provider (SP) metadata you copied when you added a new SSO connection.

    Your IdP asks for one of the following:

    • Service provider metadata URL

    • Audience URI (also called service provider sign-on callback, post-back URL, or callback URL) and the assertion consumer service URL (also called service provider entity ID or audience restriction)

Configure your SSO connection

After registering Sitecore Cloud Portal with your identity provider, you'll need to find the IdP metadata and add it to the SSO connection you created earlier.

  1. Copy the IdP metadata from your identity provider.

    Example: Ping Identity

  2. Navigate to the Sitecore Cloud Portal SSO page, find the SSO connection you created earlier, and click Configure.

  3. In Step 2: Add the identity provider metadata, select the metadata type, then paste the IdP metadata in the provided field.

  4. Click Save.

Map Sitecore user attributes to your IdP SAML attributes

When a user tries to log in, Sitecore Cloud Portal expects the required attribute: email, and the optional attributes: given_name, family_name, name, and nickname in the SAML response. Attributes are case-sensitive.

  • If your IdP's SAML response does not include the email attribute, your SSO connection will fail.

  • If the SAML response does not include family_name and first_name (or given_name) or name, your team members might have missing details in the Sitecore Cloud Portal.

Identity providers might have different names for these attributes or do not provide them by default.

If your IdP uses a different attribute name for the same information (for example emailAddress instead of email), use the Attribute mapping section of the SSO connection config to [choose the IdP SAML attribute that matches the Sitecore user attribute. / map your IdP's SAML attributes to their corresponding Sitecore user attributes / let Sitecore know the attribute name your IdP uses].

If your IdP's SAML response does not include an expected attribute or has an attribute that provides the wrong information, you must configure the attribute mapping from your IdP to return the expected attributes in the SAML response.

Test the SSO connection

Testing your SSO connection is optional but we strongly recommend it to ensure that the connection works. You can test an SSO connection at any time by trying to sign in using your identity provider.

To test the connection:

  1. Navigate to the Sitecore Cloud Portal SSO page and click Test for the connection you want to test.

  2. In the Test SSO connection dialog, click Start test.

  3. On the tab that opens, sign in using your identity provider.

If the SSO connection test failed, try again or verify that your SSO connection settings are correct.

Verify the domain

Note

You can verify the domain before or after you test the SSO connection.

To make sure that no one else uses your domain to create an SSO connection, you must verify domain ownership by adding the TXT record from the Verify domain dialog to your domain's DNS record.

To verify the domain:

  1. Navigate to the Sitecore Cloud Portal SSO page and for the connection you want to verify, click Verify domain.

  2. In the Verify domain dialog, copy the TXT record, then add it to your domain's DNS record.

    The way that you add a TXT record varies, depending on your domain host.

    In general:

    • Sign in to your domain registrar with the account and password you used to buy your domain.

    • Go to the section where you can update your domain's TXT records.Â This is typically called: DNS settings, DNS management, or advanced settings.

    • Create a new TXT record.

    For some examples, see Google's custom instructions for many popular domain registrars.

  3. Click Verify domain.

    Note

    Domain verification usually takes a few seconds. However, it can take the domain host up to 72 hours to make the TXT record publicly available.

Enable the SSO connection

After you've verified the domain and (optionally) tested your SSO connection, you can enable it.

To enable the SSO connection:

  • Navigate to the Sitecore Cloud Portal SSO page, locate the SSO connection you want to enable, and click Enable.

When you enable your SSO connection, team members that have email addresses with the email domain of an enabled SSO connection can log in using their identity provider.
